MCA barred from holding public office after being found guilty of soliciting KSh 1.7 million bribe

Publish date: 2024-03-22

- Mberia was accused of soliciting KSh 1.7 million from owners of Kiragu Waichahi School

- The court noted the prosecution provided sufficient evidence to prove the MCA received a bribe

- His co-accused, Jared Okode (MCA Mathare North Ward) and Abraham Mwangi (MCA Woodley Estate), we set free over lack of evidence

Karen ward MCA David Mberia has been jailed for three years with an option of paying KSh 700,000 after being found guilty of soliciting and receiving a bribe of KSh 1.7 million from owners of Kiragu Waichahi School.

Sufficient evidence

Mberia was accused of asking for the said amount of money to resolve a dispute surrounding a piece of land owned by former Nairobi councillor Josephat Kiragu Waichahi in Pumwani Ward, Kamukunji.

While convicting Mberia, Anti-Corruption Chief Magistrate Thomas Nzioka said investigators had supplied the court with sufficient evidence to prove beyond any reasonable doubt that the accused solicited and received the bribe.

Mberia risks losing his seat following his conviction if Section 18 of the Bribery Act will be invoked.

Co-accused freed

The other two suspects, Jared Okode (MCA Mathare North Ward) and Abraham Mwangi (MCA Woodley Estate), were let off the hook after the court ruled the prosecution failed to prove their direct involvement in the scandal.

Mberia, Okode and Mwangi were members of the Nairobi county assembly's Culture and Social Services Committee.

They reportedly demanded the bribe to make a favourable report to the Assembly to counter the initial allegation they had made that the private school in Eastleigh was illegal.

During their arrest in April 2019, detectives from Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission (EACC) recovered several bundles of notes stashed in their pockets.
