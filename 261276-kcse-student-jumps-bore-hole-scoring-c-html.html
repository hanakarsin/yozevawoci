KCSE student jumps into bore-hole after scoring C- (minus)

Publish date: 2024-07-10

- A 2017 KCSE candidate in Migori has died just hours after the results were announced

- Carren Ouma jumped into a bore-hole after she found out she had scored C- (minus)

- KCSE 2017 results were released on Wednesday, December 20

Tragedy has struck a family in Migori county after their daughter threw herself into a well following the release of the 2017 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) results.

She died.

Carren Ouma was not impressed when she learnt that she had scored a C- (minus) in the examination done in November.

Reports reaching TUKO.co.ke indicate that Carren had repeated form four with the hope of scoring grades that would see her join university.

Unfortunately for her, she managed a C- in an exam where only 142 students scored straight A.

The results were released by Education Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiangí from Nairobi School.

A girl, Naomi Kawira Karimi, from Pangani Girls emerged the best student nationally with an A of 87.011 points.

Carren's death comes after Turkana University College Principal, Professor Egara Kabaji Snr, warned parents and society against pressurising students to score As in their studies.

Kabaji said children are taken to school to learn, not to score A. He added that this is calamitous to the nation unless it is changed.
