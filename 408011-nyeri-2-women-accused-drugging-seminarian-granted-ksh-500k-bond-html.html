Nyeri: 2 Women Accused of Drugging Seminarian Granted KSh 500k Bond

Publish date: 2024-06-17

- Mercy Njeri and Rita Achieng were accused of drugging and robbing the seminarian on April 2

- The suspects were arraigned in a Nyeri court and faced charges for three counts

- Principal Magistrate James granted them KSh 500k bond with no option of bail

Two women accused of drugging seminarian have been granted KSh 500k bond by a Nyeri court with no bail option.

The suspects, Mercy Njeri and Rita Achieng, were charged before Principal Magistrate James Macharia with a count of administering Stilnox, a drug usually prescribed to people who have insomnia, to stupefy and rob the victim on April 2.

The two also faced a second count of stealing personal items, including mobile phones, laptops, and two ATM cards valued at KSh 70,000.

They also faced an alternative charge of handling stolen goods in Skuta estate, Nyeri.

According to Nation, the suspects were arrested by police on patrol who bumped into them as they left the seminarian’s house at around 3am.

Nyeri central sub-county police commander Paul Kuria said that after interrogating the women, they discovered that the two were responsible for spiking drinks in bars.

“They were interrogated, and some drugs, documents, cell phones and other items they could not account for were recovered. Reports had earlier been made by people who said they had been drugged. On further interrogation, we realised the women were behind the spiking of drinks in bars,” Kuria said.

Kuria added that the interrogation revealed a more sinister operation by the duo, leading to another woman's arrest at a hideout in the area.

Six stolen phones, ATM cards, and Stilnox tablets, a drug usually prescribed to people who have insomnia, were recovered from the house.

The seminarian arrived at his home at around 7pm in the company of the two women and a male friend.

Surveillance footage showed that they entered the house at 7.38pm.

They were seen drinking and smoking as they engage in a lively conversation. An hour later, the host passes out on the living room floor as the women rummaged through the house. It was still unclear how and when the male friend left.

For the next two hours, they are seen going through the man’s belongings and occasionally sitting down to smoke.

In a separate story reported by TUKO.co.ke, three women were arrested in Nyeri county for allegedly drugging and robbing men in bars in Ruringu.

Rita Achieng Okuto, Caroline Kariuki and Caroline Wanjiku were also arrested for being out past curfew time against the president's directive issued on Friday, March 25, to curb the spread of the virus.
