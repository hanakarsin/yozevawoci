Nyeri: Driver of Late Tycoon Accused of Killing Son Sentenced to 2 Years in Jail

Publish date: 2024-05-05

- Stephen Wang'donu allegedly hired killers at the cost of KSh 160,000 to murder his son Daniel Mwangi

- His driver James Mahinda confessed to transporting hitmen to Mwangi's home, where he was killed

- Justice Muchemi noted that Mahinda was acting on firm instructions of someone who had influence over him

A former driver who was arrested in connection with the murder of his employer's son has signed a plea bargain agreement with the prosecutors.

Nyeri tycoon Stephen Wang’ondu's driver, James Mahinda, had pleaded guilty to the lesser offence of neglect in the offence of murder of Daniel Mwangi in January 2021.

Although Nyeri High Court judge Florence Muchemi agreed that Mahinda was acting on firm instructions of Wang'ondu, the main suspect, she noted the court had a duty to punish such offenders.

Lady justice Muchemi handed Mahinda two years imprisonment and another one year for probation.

The court heard that the businessman had instructed Mahinda to transport hitmen Eddy Ngari and Raphael Kariuki to Mwangi's home to execute his dark plan.

“The court also considers that he is a young man of 38 years with a family of two, with his youngest child being two years old,” she said.

At the same time, the prosecution team led by Duncan Ondimu withdrew the case against another named Geoffrey Warutumo after detectives failed to place him at the murder scene.

Detectives established that Warutumo was in Embu county when the plot to kill the deceased was being executed and planned in Nyeri.

“In fact, on the fateful day when the plot was executed, the suspect did not leave Embu county yet the incident occurred in the Mwiyogo area in Nyeri,” he said.

Stephen Wang’ondu sudden death

The suspect was set free by Muchemi on Wednesday, April 28, alongside the four co-accused.

However, Wang’ondu was pronounced dead at the Outspan Hospital in Nyeri on May 8.

Detectives said the late businessman paid hitmen KSh 160,000 to kill his son so he could claim insurance compensation after the murder.
