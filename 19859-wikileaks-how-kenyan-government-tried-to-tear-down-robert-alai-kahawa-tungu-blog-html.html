How Kenyan Government Tried To 'Tear Down' Kahawa Tungu

Publish date: 2024-07-24

Kenya security officials wanted to acquire a Italian surveillance software that could access and attack communication gadgets remotely, Wikileaks has revealed.

According to e-mails on the website, the Italian company Hacking Team was first contacted by a Nairobi based communication firm Com 21 Ltd in 2014 presumably on behalf of the government.

Emad Shehata, the company's account manager in charge of Kenya, then sent back an email detailing what their Remote Control System, named Galileo, could do.

He said the system could also access all the information from smartphones including: Skype calls, Facebook, Twitter, WhatsApp, Line and Viber.

Chris Kinyajui, Com 21 Ltd director, then connected the Hacking Team to Office of the President's cyber security department in order to start the acquisition process.

The two sides exchanged a series of mails and by May 6, 2015, they seemed to have reached a deal.

The government officials then allegedly requested to have Kahawa Tungu, a blog associated with blogger Robert Alai, brought down in order to 'serve as a great proof of concept' and capability.

Daniele Milan, the Hacking Team's operations manager, declined to grant the request citing company's policy and international regulations.

According to cyber security expert Lendsey Achudi, a system similar to Galileo could cost up to $800 million.

The National Intelligence Service said Kenya is not a Hacking Team's client.
