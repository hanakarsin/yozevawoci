Renowned Industrialist Chris Kirubi Dies Aged 80, Family Confirms

Publish date: 2024-03-06
  • Chris Kirubi's family confirmed the death of their patriarch on Monday, June 14
  • The businessman had a long battle with cancer
  • He has died aged 80 years and was the chairman of the Nairobi-based Capital FM, and the director of Centum Investment

Renowned Nairobi industrialist Christopher John Kirubi is dead, TUKO.co.ke can report.

Kirubi, who had been in and out of the hospital, had a long battle with cancer since 2017 when he was diagnosed with the ailment.

The news of his demise was rife on social media on Monday, June 14, before his family later confirmed the sad news.

"It is with great sadness that we announce the passing of Dr Christopher J. Kirubi (1941-2021) who passed away today, Monday the 14th of June, 2021 at 1pm at his home after a long battle with cancer fought with fortitude, grace and courage. He was surrounded by his family," a statement from the family read.

More details to be released by the family in the coming days.

By the time of his death, the Harvard Business School alumnus served as the director at Centum Investment and the chairman of Capital Group Limited that owns Capital FM.

He was also a proprietor of a handful of other businesses and properties including DHL Express Kenya Limited, Haco Industries Kenya Limited, and Kiruma International Limited.

Earlier on the day, as was his routine, Kirubi took to his social media handles to give motivational nuggets to his followers.

However, his advice on Monday seemed to mirror his demise, given the premonitory parting shot on it.

"Top of the morning to you, my dear friend. It doesn’t have to be a tiring Monday. Instead, I bring to you good tidings of favour, success, achievements and happiness in the new week. Give yourself a go towards your success plan. Enjoy today and the rest of your week. #AskKirubi," read Kirubi's last message on the morning of Monday, June 14.

Kenyans mourn Kirubi

Kenyans trooped the interwebs to pay tribute to the businessman who stamped his mark in the corporate world.

Almost every sector of the economy had a touch of the fallen octogenarian.

"Chris Kirubi was my friend and one to many others and a mentor to even more. He was a dedicated entrepreneur and philanthropist who helped scores of Kenyans over time. My heartfelt condolences go to his family, friends and all those whose lives he touched. Rest In Peace Chris," said ODM chief Raila Odinga."My condolences go to the family of late, Chris Kirubi. He was an illustrious businessman who spoke candidly on matters of national importance and the economy. A true patriot who told you as it is; without blinking. Over and above that he was witty, humorous and an avid golfer," ANC party leader Musalia Mudavadi."Go well Chris Kirubi, a good and amiable man. You'll be greatly missed. RIP," Senator Millicent Omanga.
