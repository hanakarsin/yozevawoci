Kipchoge, Lupita and Tabichi named among 100 most influential Africans by New African Magazine

- Lupita Nyong'o, Trevor Noah and Peter Tabichi are among the notable African ersonalities who have left a mark in people's lives

- Lupita together with her mother Dorothy Nyong'o were awarded for the efforts in creating cancer awareness

- Peter was named as the best teacher across the globe and awarded with a whopping KSh 100 million

Kenya has time and again been termed as a hub of talent, gifts and remarkable skill sets.

On top of that, the country is praised for its unique and picturesque holiday destinations.

Thanks to notable personalities like the world’s best teacher Peter Tabichi and seasoned international actress Lupita Nyong’o, Kenya’s flag continues to fly high across the globe.

Notably, three personalities from the country made it to the top 100 list of Africans who impacted lives across the globe with their art, talent and wits.

Lupita went head to head with the likes of Rwanda’s president Paul Kagame, Ugandan politician Bobi Wine, South African singer Sho Madjozi and Ethiopia’s President Abiy Ahmed.

New African Magazine termed them as influential Africans who have “asserted their rights and put the interests of people ahead of their own.”

Here are some of the achievements and efforts of Africa’s popular celebrities:

1.Lupita Nyong’o.

The actress and her mother Dorothy Nyong’o have been hailed for their role in creating cancer awareness.

The two received a prestigious award from Harlem School of Arts on Monday, October 21.

2.Paul Kagame.

The Rwandan President has helped rewrite his country’s story and revolutionised it from a war-torn state to a desirable destination both for tourists and aspiring investors.

Under his rule, the country has made significant strides in infrastructure and technology.

3.Peter Tabichi.

The Kenyan teacher from Nauru county was awarded KSh 100 million in Dubai for his kind efforts.

The Mathematics and Physics teacher contributes 80% of his salary towards the education of needy students.

Since his big win, the educator has brushed shoulders with the likes of US President Donald Trump and even had the rare opportunity to pray during the opening of the US Congress.

4.Trevor Noah.

The celebrated comedian started small and was first based in South Africa. Later, he bagged himself a lucrative job in the US as a big time TV show host.

Ever since, he has been nominated for different awards and also been named as one of the best paid comedians.

5.Sho Madjozi.

The singer quickly rose to fame after releasing a rap song dubbed John Cena and even being lucky enough to meet the decorated wrestler. Despite being South African, Sho raps perfectly in Kiswahili and has created a sense of integration between her fans.

6. Eliud Kipchoge.

The super marathoner proved indeed no human is limited after smashing the INEOS 1:59 challenge and ran 42km under two hours.
