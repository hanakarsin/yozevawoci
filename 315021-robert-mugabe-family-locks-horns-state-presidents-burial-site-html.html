Robert Mugabe: Family locks horns with state over former president's burial site

Publish date: 2024-04-28

- Remains of the late former head of state was expected to leave Singapore on Wednesday

- Mugabe will be laid to rest over the coming weekend according to the government

-There was, however, no clear communication on where the founding president would be buried

- Mugabe who was president of Zimbabwe for close to four decades died on Friday after a long illness

Body of Zimbabwe president Robert Mugabe is expected to arrive home from Singapore on Wednesday, September 11, ahead of burial over the coming weekend.

The Zimbabwean government indicated a funeral service of the founding president will be held at Harare National Sports stadium on Saturday and invited members of the public, local and foreign leaders to grace the important occasion.

There, however, seems to be a sharp disagreement between the family and the government on where the nonagenarian should be laid to rest according to The Telegram and other international news outlets.

Sources privy to the funeral arrangement revealed whereas the state wanted Mugabe to be laid to rest at the cemetery for heroes of the liberation war at the Heroes Acre, next to his first wife Sally's grave, some family members want him interred at his village home in Zvimba district, northwest of the capital city.

The late ex-president's cousin, Josephine Jorincha, who TUKO.co.ke understands was in charge of the burial arrangements, told the media the village chiefs were locked in negotiations with the state over the burial site.

He was quoted saying the old man's wish was that his body be laid to rest at his home in the village and not at the Heroes’ Acre as was suggested by the state.

Mugabe, who ruled the country for 37 years from 1980 to 2017 when he was ousted following a military coup, died on Friday, September 6, aged 95 after a long illness.

He passed on in Singapore where he had been receiving treatment since April 2019.
