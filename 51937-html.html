Ibadan Youths Laud MTN For A Great Show As Loud in Naija Concert Begins

Publish date: 2024-04-16

Youths turned out, in droves, to witness one of the best concerts ever held in Ibadan, last weekend, at the Adamasingba Stadium, as MTN’s ambassadors and musical superstars stormed the city for a most thrilling musical ‘feast’ tagged ‘Loud in Naija’.

Featuring artistes like Wizkid, Davido, KCEE, Tiwa Savage, Chidinma, Sound Sultan, Waconzy, Praiz and many more, the concert was a convergence of industry entertainers.

The fans were treated to sensational performances by the artistes and they didn’t hide their feelings. Kunle Fagbamigbe, a resident of Ibadan, expressed his joy at being part of the great show. According to him, ‘it is good to have these artistes come to perform, at this season of the year, especially, shortly, after some of them have just released new singles and albums, like KCEE’s “Okoso”’. 

Participants, in the karaoke session, that held at the concert were glad to have been granted such an opportunity to share the stage with their favourite artistes, as well as take pictures and receive autographs.

The female fans were, particularly, excited. Most of them came with placards bearing the images of the artistes and messages of endearment. Speaking to some of them, they appreciated the fact that MTN brought such a high-profile concert to their city.

Almost all the artistes engaged the audience, in different forms of dances, starting from ‘skelewu’, the latest dance style. Davido, the originator of the dance, was a class act. Brimming with exceptional youthful energy and a unique voice, the ‘Omo-baba-olowo’ left his Ibadan fans with a good memory.

Sound Sultan, one of the music industry’s most consistent artistes, did justice to the status of his new single, ‘Natural Something’. His performance drew a huge applause from the crowd who kept dancing and asking for more.

Meanwhile, MTN Project Fame, the telecommunications and ICT company’s platform for nurturing young talented musicians, made an invaluable contribution to the concert. Being the most popular talent-hunt initiative in West Africa, the platform contributed no fewer than six artistes to the concert. Chidinma, Praiz, iMike, Monica, Ayo, Oyinkansade and Adetoun  had a remarkable evening and Ibadan youths appreciated their performances as applauses greeted each of these artiste’s showmanship on stage.

Wizkid, a favourite, among Ibadan youths, was thrilled with the large turn-out. He said the passion he witnessed from the Ibadan crowd was unbelievable. In his words – ‘they love a good show’.

The MTN ‘Loud in Naija’ musical concert will be held in five more cities in Nigeria. The cities are Port Harcourt, Abuja, Ilorin, Owerri and Lagos. All these cities will be covered within the next few weeks.
