Emotional photos as Churchill Show comedian Njenga Mswahili is buried

Publish date: 2024-04-24

The body of late Churchill Show comedian Anthony Njenga, known to many as Njenga Mswahili, has been moved from the City Mortuary where it was being preserved to the Lang'ata Cemetery, ready for burial, TUKO.co.ke has learnt.

The body was moved from the morgue on Thursday, November 14, morning, about six days after Njenga's sad demise.

The latest development was first made public by late Njenga's fellow comedian Mwende who shared photos from the mortuary and a minor memorial mass held on Thursday morning.

''On our way to give our friend and workmate and an amazing comedian a peaceful sendoff Njenga Mswahili, RIP my friend," she captioned photos of comedians carrying the deceased's body from the morgue.

TUKO.co.ke reached out to comedian Chipukeezy for details and he confirmed Njenga will be buried at the Lang'ata cemetery on Thursday, November 14, in the afternoon.

''The burial process is currently underway at the Lang'ata cemetery,'' Chipu said on phone.

Tens of comedians drawn from the Churchill Show and others converged at the morgue to pick his body and together headed to for the interment.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke earlier, Njenga was found dead on Thursday, November 7, in Ndonyo Market, Dagoretti South, after allegedly being hit by a train.

Although the exact cause of his death was not immediately clear, it was alleged he was going through a rough time and may have ended his life.

Speaking exclusively to TUKO.co.ke, Erick Kiogora, another former Churchill show comedian, revealed Njenga must have been knocked down knowingly or unknowingly by a train in the wee hours of Thursday morning, around the time trains usually pass through the area.

He also disclosed the late was unrecognisable and his mother had to confirm that indeed it was him.

"We couldn't really say if it was him, but when the mother came, she immediately confirmed that it was her son," a dejected Erick said.
