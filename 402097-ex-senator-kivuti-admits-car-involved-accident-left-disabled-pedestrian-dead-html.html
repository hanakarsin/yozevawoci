Ex-senator Kivuti admits his car was involved in accident that left disabled pedestrian dead

Publish date: 2024-07-13

- Chris Nderitu was hit and killed by a convoy of politicians who were heading to Sagana State Lodge for a meeting with President Uhuru Kenyatta

- Details of the cars that ran over the disabled man have been scanty since the accident occurred a week ago

- Former Embu senator Lenny Kivuti has admitted his car was one of the three that were involved in an accident

Former Embu senator Lenny Kivuti has admitted his car was one of the three that were involved in an accident that killed a disabled man in Nyeri.

Kivuti, however, dismissed reports that it was his vehicle that killed 45-yea-old Chris Nderitu who was by the roadside when the accident occurred on Saturday, January 30.

The former senator was part of the convoy that was heading to Sagana State Lodge to attend President Uhuru Kenyatta's meeting with Mt Kenya leaders.

"My car was involved in the accident but it did not kill the victim," Kivuti told Daily Nation.

Eyewitnesses said the first car hit Nderitu off his wheelchair while the next two which were following at a close range ran over the disabled man.

Hit-and-run

While initial reports indicated the cars sped off after the tragic incident, the former lawmaker said they stopped and allowed police officers who were part of the convoy to secure the scene and record all the needed information.

The politician noted the three drivers also remained at the scene of the accident to help the officers with investigation.

"It is not true to say the victim was killed by a hit-and-run vehicle...we stopped and allowed the OCPD and officers who were part of the convoy to do what they could have done in such a situation," he added.

A witness who said was walking beside Nderitu before tragedy struck identified the killer car as a black Toyota Landcruiser.

It is this testimony that led independent investigators to identify Kivuti's car as the potential killer.

Not aware

On Thursday, February 3, Police Spokesman Charles Owino said he was not aware of the VIP car than ran over the disabled man.

He, however, noted police officers were investigating the matter and assured the family of justice.

"The family should rest assured that we shall follow procedures strictly no matter which vehicle was involved in crushing the person,” he said.

Nderitu was buried on Friday, February 5.
