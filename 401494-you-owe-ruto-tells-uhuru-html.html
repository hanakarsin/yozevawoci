You owe me nothing, Ruto tells Uhuru

Publish date: 2024-06-06

- Deputy President William Ruto agreed that President Uhuru Kenyatta does not owe him anything as he had earlier said

- Ruto said since they were elected jointly, they only had a debt of gratitude to Kenyans

- The head of state had stated he does not owe his deputy any political debt despite their famous "kumi ya Uhuru, kumi ya Ruto" pact

Deputy President William Ruto has responded to his boss, President Uhuru Kenyatta and agreed with him that he does not owe him any political debt.

During a consultative meeting with over 7,000 Mt Kenya leaders at the Sagana State Lodge on Saturday, January 30, the head of state stated he does not owe his deputy any political debt despite their famous "kumi ya Uhuru, kumi ya Ruto" pact.

Speaking on Sunday, January 31, in Narok county, the second in command reiterated Uhuru neither owes him nor any community any political debt maintaining that they only have a debt of gratitude to Kenyans.

"I want to say for the avoidance of doubt, that I supported my friend Uhuru Kenyatta not because of his community, not because of the language he speaks. And as President Kenyatta said yesterday correctly, he has no debt to nobody. He doesn't have my debt. Uhuru Kenyatta owes me nothing, he owes no community anything. There is only one debt that I share with Uhuru Kenyatta and that is the debt of uniting this country," he said.

Ethnic politics

In what seemed like a thin-veiled attack on the president's Sagana meeting with Mt Kenya politicians, Ruto also said he could not backslide to the politics of ethnicity which he said was toxic to Kenyans.

"As the deputy president, I understand the folly of ethnic bigotry and tribal zealotry. I will not be invited to the politics of ethnic mobilisation and tribal arrangements."

Earlier, his allies had dismissed Uhuru's consultative meeting in Sagana as ethnic mobilisation meant to benefit the privileged few.

Ruto's key allies took issue with Uhuru for badmouthing the DP and his hustler nation for his growing influence in the president's backyard.

Kandara lawmaker Alice Wahome, Ruto's key point woman in the Mt Kenya region, said Uhuru's meeting with Central Kenya politicians could only be interpreted as ethnic mobilisation.

"I have listened to several video clips of H.E Uhuru Kenyatta. It’s ethnic mobilization. It’s regrettable. What is ailing this President? Ten years and you are still complaining," she tweeted.
