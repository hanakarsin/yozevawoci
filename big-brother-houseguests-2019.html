Is Julie Chen hosting Big Brother 21?

Yes! Despite everyone's fear that Julie would not be hosting Big Brother amid her husband Les Moonves' sexual harassment scandal, she has made it clear that she will be returning for the 21st season of the hit reality show.

In addition to sharing a slew of different photos leading up to the cast reveal, including peeking her head outside the door of the house, Julie also shared a photo of all the cast members on Monday.

In September, Julie stepped down from The Talk after nine years, which made fans worried she wouldn't return to Big Brother. But even during the public embarrassment that surrounded her following the allegations against her husband, she finished out Season 20 of Big Brother and even hosted the show's spin-off, Celebrity Big Brother, over the winter. 

Then, in May, she posted a clip of the Season 21 premiere info with the caption. "Anyone else ready for Summer? #BB21 is coming!" Um yes, Julie, we are...

When does Big Brother 21 premiere?

Big Brother 21 premieres on Tuesday, June 25 at 8 p.m. EST. The second part of the premiere will air on Wednesday, June 26 at 8 p.m. EST. 

After that, the show typically keeps a Sunday and Wednesday at 8 p.m. EST and Thursday at 9 p.m. schedule.

Where can I watch Big Brother 21?

Big Brother 21 will always air on CBS, but if you can't catch it live (we won't judge you, the show airs three times a week, so it's a huge commitment), don't fret. You can watch full episodes on CBS All Access the next day.

With CBS All Access, you can even stream live feeds and see the houseguests at all times of the day and night.

And if you're really a Big Brother superfan, you can watch Big Brother After Dark on Pop every night for an even better behind-the-scenes look at what's going on in the house, including all the juicy happenings that don't make the primetime episodes.
