Bernadette Wangui Ng'ang'a: Meet phenomenal woman eyeing Nairobi women rep seat in 2022

Publish date: 2024-05-19

- Bernadette Wangui Ng'ang'a was on the ballot in 2017 but despite losing, she's ready to make another attempt

- The relentless politician believes she has something great to offer Nairobians, especially for the women and youth

- She vows to become an accessible leader who will come up with need-based solutions for city dwellers

In the 2017 elections, Bernadette Wangui Ng'ang'a unsuccessfully contested for the Nairobi woman representative but that did not erase the vision she has for the city.

It is this particular vision and dream that is taking Wangui to the ballot again in 2022 as she believes she has something great to offer to the city dwellers.

Speaking exclusively to TUKO.co.ke, the politician said leadership comes from God. She believes she has a divine purpose for Nairobi. That is why she will, once again, seek the opportunity to serve.

"I believe I have what it takes to make a positive impact for the Nairobi women and youth. I will be vying in Nairobi because I am a resident of Nairobi and as they say, charity begins at home," she said.

The mother of two does not give up easily and firmly believes she can elevate the lives of Nairobians if given a chance to lead.

"When you fail, you do not give up, you rise and try again until you succeed. There is nothing more gratifying than overcoming a challenge successfully," she said.

An accessible leader

Wangui promises to be a leader who the women and youth would be able access easily to offer solutions to problems in a more meaningful manner.

She emphasised the importance of need-based solutions and a bottom-up approach system of governance which will be one of her guiding principles if she becomes a leader.

On finances and campaigns, the aspirant revealed she will use her small savings from her business and does not mind getting support from friends, family and well-wishers for her campaigns in 2022.

As the world celebrated women from all spheres during this year's International Women's Day, Wangui sent a shout out to all women saying they should always believe in themselves because they mean so much to the world.

"Women should believe and accept themselves in their own skin, hold their heads up high and face the world," she said.

BBI politics

Wangui hailed the proposed amendments in the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) saying the documents will actually bring more goodies to Kenyans.

"BBI will bring more opportunities for women and youth. The new constituencies will give more women the opportunity to vie hence more chances for women in leadership opportunities," she said.

She argued the tax grace period for the youths in the BBI is a positive development that all youngsters should embrace.
