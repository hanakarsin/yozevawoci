Tonto Dikeh's biography

Tonto Dikeh was born on June 9th, 1985, in Port Harcourt as Tonto Wigo Charity Dikeh. Her parents are Sunny Dikeh (father) and Veronica Dikeh (mother).

She is the third born in a family of five children.

The actress lost her mother when she was three years old. Her father then remarried, and her stepmother raised her since she was young.

How old is Tonto Dikeh now?

As of 2021, the actress is 36 years old.

Education

After completing high school, the actress joined River State University of Science and Technology. She graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Petrochemical Engineering.

Acting career

Tonto had a passion for acting since she was young. She began her career in the entertainment industry in 2005, after appearing on the reality television program The Next Movie Star.

She won the competition and was allowed to register for the Actors Guild of Nigeria. Her first role was in 2006 in the film Tea and Coffee.

Tonto Dikeh's wedding ceremony

Is Tonto Dikeh married? Tonto was previously married to Olakunle Oladunni Churchill. Tonto Dikeh's ex-husband is a nephew of the former Nigerian president Olusegun Obasanjo.

Olakunle Churchill and Tonto's relationship blossomed quickly when they met in a Lagos nightclub. The two got married in 2015. Tonto Dikeh's traditional marriage was held in her home town in River State, Nigeria.

Where was Tonto Dikeh's white wedding held? The couple did not have a white wedding ceremony. They only did the traditional wedding, which was later explained as necessary as she was pregnant at that time.

The actress parted ways with Churchill in 2017, just two years after their wedding, over allegations of domestic abuse and extra-marital affairs.

Tonto Dikeh's child

Churchill and Tonto were blessed with a son who was born on 17th February, 2016. Tonto Dikeh's son's name is King Andre Dikeh.

Tonto Dikeh's surgery

Tonto Dikeh is one of those who don't shy about having undergone a plastic surgery procedure to fix her figure. The mother of one said she underwent the procedure to alter her physique since she knew she didn't have a curvy figure.

During an interview she stated that,

“Almost everyone knows, before now, that my physique wasn’t always right. If I had denied undergoing plastic surgery, fans would come trolling me for not revealing the truth."

The actress added that,

"There’s also the stereotype problem that makes African women who undergo the procedure feel they have to deny doing so and keep it a secret. But why would I be ashamed to say I altered my physique? That norm, I think I’ve broken away from it. Confidence! Since I had my bum done, it has bolstered my confidence. I liked myself more. I became happier such that I even doubted if this was truly my body. If you don’t like it, fix it. That’s me."

Tonto Dikeh's foundation

She established an NGO - Tonto Dikeh Foundation, which helps women fight against injustice and offers them psychological help.

Apart from this, the organization also takes care of abuse victims and awards scholarships to students with financial problems. The actress has also helped the Makurdi flood victims financially.
