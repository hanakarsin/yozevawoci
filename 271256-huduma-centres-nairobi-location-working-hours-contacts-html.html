Huduma centres in Nairobi: location, working hours and contacts

Publish date: 2024-05-04

Huduma Kenya is a flagship programme launched by the President of the Republic of Kenya on 7th November, 2013. The programme is for the betterment of the access and delivery of the Government of Kenya’s services to their citizens. It’s a Vision 2030 flagship project launched for the reformation of service delivery in Kenya. If you want to know more about the Huduma centres in Nairobi then you may explore this article. It will be an exciting journey. Huduma centres in Nairobi bring everything related to public services under the same roof. This is so that each and every Kenyan citizen can access these services easily and conveniently. Huduma centres provide the necessary documents and important information to the service seekers. Huduma centres are the enhanced providers of public service Kenya.

Huduma centre services

Huduma centre offers efficient government services to the citizens of Kenya in the most convenient way possible. Some of the services provided by Huduma Kenya are:

  • Issuing birth certificates. For Huduma centre birth certificate, one needs to provide the birth notification document.
  • Issuing initial identity cards (Documents Required – birth certificate and a copy of it, identity cards of parents and a copy of the same).
  • Assessing stamp duty for all transfers ranging from shares to land (Documents Required – supporting documents like sale agreement, MOA’s, and AOA’s e.t.c, and Form B).
  • Registering self-help groups, welfare societies, and community-based organizations (CBOs) (Documents Required – completed registration forms, list of members along with their ID’s, name of the welfare society, Form A & B completely filled up).
  • Those who intend to start a business needs to register the name of his/her business with the Huduma Centres (Documents Required – name of his/her business and completely filled up BN2 form).
  • Registration of private/limited companies (Documents Required – company name, MOA, AOA).
  • Registration of members of NSSF (Documents Required – national ID cards of members).
  • Registering an employer (owner of a company) with the NSSF (National Social Security Fund) (Documents required – Form SF 001, Incorporation Certificate and Trading License).
  • Clearance certificate from police (Documents required – national identity card).
  • Registration of members of NHIF both informal as well informal sector (Documents Required – formal Sector – Introductory Letter provided by the employer, ID card copy, colored passport size photo, if he/she is married then copy of his/her spouse’s ID card and his/her colored passport size photo and again if they have children then his/her passport size photos and birth certificates).
  • Issuance of tax compliance certificate/clearance certificate (Documents Required – Completed I-TAX form).
  • Providing information regarding status of vehicle registration process through logbook search (Documents Required – registration number of the vehicle).
  • Renewing driver’s license in online mode (Documents Required – name of the person, his ID number).
  • Complaint receival cell for redressing the issues related to services delivered by the Huduma centre (Documents required – national ID card number, related documents).
  • Issuing self declaration forms (No documents required).
  • Providing status of pension claim (Documents required – personal number/ ID number).
  • Inquiring about certificates and cards (No documents required).
  • Issuing single business permit (Documents required – a copy of national identity card, name or a copy of company’s registration certificate, the physical address of the business entity).
  • Providing liquor license (authority letter from Liquor Board).
